Scientific writing can become defensive very quickly. It hides behind density, assumes too much, and sometimes mistakes difficulty for seriousness. I am more interested in work that remains precise while still letting a reader move through it with confidence.

## Clarity is not simplification

Clear writing does not mean flattening an idea until nothing demanding remains. It means arranging the path well enough that the reader understands where the difficulty really is.

Three habits seem especially important:

- name the question before expanding the context
- separate evidence from interpretation
- keep claims proportional to what the work can actually show

## Explanation has a moral dimension

Part of the responsibility of research is to avoid unnecessary obscurity. If a method matters, it should be possible to describe why. If a result is uncertain, that uncertainty should remain visible rather than being hidden by tone.

This is one reason I want writing and research to remain close together on the site. The archive is not just a place for polished conclusions. It is also a place for learning how to think in public more responsibly.
